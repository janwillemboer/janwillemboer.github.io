Rubric Requirements Engineering for SE (Draft)

Insufficient Poor Sufficient Good Excellent
Stakeholders (5) No stakeholder analysis or quality too low (below sufficient) There is a stakeholder analysis in which the role and importance of the important stakeholders in the project are explained and argued (between sufficient and excellent) There is a stakeholder analysis in which the role and importance of all stakeholders in the project are explained and argued. There is also a power influence matrix.
Requirement elicitation (10) The important requirements are missing or quality too low (below sufficient) The minimum set of requirements has been found (between sufficient and excellent) All requirements have been found, it is clear where the requirements come from. Additional, useful requirements have been advised and argued, for example based on the ISO25010 standard.
Documenting the requirements (10) Requirements have not been elaborated or quality too low (below sufficient) The most important requirements have been elaborated in a SMART way. (between sufficient and excellent) All requirements are elaborated in a SMART way, logically arranged (e.g. arranged by importance), correctly numbered and classified, and without spelling or grammar errors
Functional design (10) No FD or quality too low (below sufficient) The components of the portfolio have a logical place in the functional design. There are wireframes with explanations for the most important parts of the application. (between sufficient and excellent) All parts of the portfolio have a logical place in the functional design. There is a clear introduction, which makes the reason + goal clear. Wireframes with explanations give a clear picture of the flow and details of all parts of the application. The document has a satisfying conclusion with, for example, the next steps. The texts reviewed here are well written and do not contain spelling or grammar errors.
Product backlog (10) No backlog or quality too low (below sufficient) There is a backlog with the titles of the most important functionalities, sorted by priority. The items of the prototype have been elaborated so that a development team can get started with this. (between sufficient and excellent) There is a backlog with the titles of all functionalities, with estimated weight, sorted by priority. The items selected for the prototype have been elaborated in such a way that a development team can get started. The elaborated items comply with I(N)VEST. The elaborations do not contain spelling or grammar errors. For the elaborated items it is clear which requirements they belong to.
Tests (10) No testcases or quality too low (below sufficient) The test cases cover the most important requirements. They consist of concrete steps that can be carried out by a tester. (between sufficient and excellent) The test cases cover all requirements of the prototype. They consist of concrete steps that can be carried out by a tester without technical or content knowledge. It is clear how the tests cover the requirements. The tests are repeatable.
Prototype (25) No prototype and no tests, or quality too low (below sufficient) There is an application in JavaFX or AppInventor in which the most important functionalities are implemented according to the design. There is at least 1 test report that proves this. (between sufficient and excellent) There is an application in JavaFX or AppInventor in which all backlog items selected for the prototype are implemented according to the design. There is at least 1 test report in which the test cases have been executed on the created application. The test report unmistakably proves that the app is complete, made to specification, and works.
Review No review or quality too low (below sufficient) The review covers the important parts of the portfolio with meaningful comments and suggestions. (between sufficient and excellent) The review covers all the parts with well-written expert comments and concrete suggestions for:
  • stakeholder analysis
  • completeness of the requirements
  • elaboration of the requirements
  • the layout and parts of the functional design that are not already found elsewhere
  • the product backlog
  • the tests
  • the prototype on the basis of the tests.
